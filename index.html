Executive summary

The proposal, in plain terms

In one breath: Filecoin is finally getting paid for storage — but the money is paid in stablecoins and never reaches FIL, the token that secures the network. As paid usage grows, FIL value capture doesn't. We fix that with a real product.

The problem

When a client pays for Filecoin storage today, the payment settles to the provider in a stablecoin (USDFC/USDC). FIL is barely touched. Every existing way FIL gains value is tied to capacity and funded by shrinking token emissions — not by paid demand. So the network can succeed commercially while the token fails. (Separately, Filecoin advertises service guarantees — SLAs — that aren't actually funded.)

The idea

Turn paid storage into FIL value through a product people actually want: a funded SLA-insurance market.

  • Storage providers pay a small premium to promise clients guaranteed service — backed by real capital, not marketing.
  • That premium buys and burns FIL (makes it scarcer) and pays FIL stakers who insure the guarantee (gives FIL a real income stream).
  • Result: better service for clients, real demand for FIL, and the network's #1 KPI — paid usage — finally accrues to the token.

Why it's strong (and different)

  • Not a token gimmick — value capture is the byproduct of a product (funded SLAs), so there's a real reason providers pay.
  • Not circular — the money comes from outside the FIL system, the proven MakerDAO/Sky & Hyperliquid model.
  • It ships — live on mainnet in 6 months; works with any provider and any stablecoin; builds on what already exists (Filecoin Pay, USDFC, Uniswap v3) instead of reinventing it.
  • A public good — open-source, contributed as a standard (FIP), community-governed.
The ask · one-sentence pitch $300,000 · 6 months · milestone-based. The FIL Value Engine makes Filecoin's growing paid revenue flow back into FIL — by turning service guarantees into a funded insurance market whose premiums burn FIL and pay the stakers who back them.

Method

How to read this

This document is the argument, not a pitch. We descended a ladder — from the strategic to the tactical — and refused to skip a rung. Every claim is grounded in primary sources (Filecoin spec, FIPs, the Filecoin Pay source code, market data) and held to one standard: be skeptical first. Where the popular narrative was hype, we said so; where Filecoin's own pitch overclaimed, we flagged it.

The path: understand the 2026 strategy → test whether its objectives are right against the real state of the ecosystem → locate Filecoin's defensible place in the data-infrastructure market → pressure-test the fashionable wedges (AI provenance, RWA) → re-derive the objectives as a turnaround CEO would → isolate the one existential question (does paid demand reach FIL?) → decode the grant program → learn exactly how the payment machinery works → and only then design a mechanism and model its economics.

The thesis in one line Filecoin's survival hinges on converting paid demand into FIL value — and today it doesn't. The mechanism that fixes this is not a token sink but a funded SLA-insurance market whose premiums burn FIL and pay FIL stakers who underwrite service quality.
Part I

The 2026 strategy: away from supply, toward paid demand

Filecoin's official 2026 strategy is an explicit pivot — "away from growing supply and towards scaling demand." Three pillars: (1) drive paid, on-chain deals; (2) strengthen network profitability & cryptoeconomics; (3) scale paid, flagship client adoption. The repeated phrase is "paid, onchain." The token-economics line is the tell: "the final network vesting periods will end… redirect network rewards towards paid usage."

NotableThe strategy never mentions "burn/lock." It talks about redirecting rewards toward paid usage — yet the RFP places us in "Area 4: Burn & Lock." Reconciling that tension became a thread we pull throughout.

Part IV

The fashionable wedges, tested skeptically

AI data provenance

The headline AI wedge largely dissolves under scrutiny. Provenance does not need a blockchain — C2PA / Content Credentials (PKI, explicitly no blockchain) plus transparency logs already solve attestation, and ship in the Pixel 10, Sony/Leica cameras, and OpenAI's output. Crucially, Filecoin proves possession, not origin: PDP attests "these bytes are held intact," never "who created them." Selling Filecoin proofs as provenance is a category error. Demand is nascent — competitors (0G ~$290M, Story ~$140M) are louder, and the DataDAO momentum went to a non-Filecoin chain.

Real-world-asset (RWA) tokenization

A token is structurally insufficient — RWAs need a persistent, verifiable data layer (legal docs, proof-of-reserves, NAV, retention per SEC 17a-4 / MiFID). But the market already met the part it values (NAV/reserves) with oracles (Chainlink publishes numbers, not documents), and decentralized-storage adoption in institutional RWA is ~zero. The live IPFS pattern (RealT) is the cautionary tale, not the proof.

The recurring patternIn every wedge — egress, AI provenance, RWA — Filecoin wins the same narrow layer (verifiable persistence) and always beneath a value/attestation layer it doesn't own (oracles, C2PA, issuers). That is itself the central strategic finding: don't sell the wedge; sell the persistence substrate — and find where value can accrue to FIL.
Part V

Are the objectives even right? A CEO's reframe

The official objectives nail the diagnosis but read like an aspiration deck: three overlapping demand goals, a cryptoeconomics pillar that ducks the one existential question, no chosen wedge, no stated sacrifices, and — explicitly — no targets. As a turnaround CEO we'd compress them into four sharper ones:

  • O1 — Win one wedge decisively (verifiable, egress-free persistence — not the AI-provenance hype). Concede hot/AI-IO and general cloud.
  • O2 — Make FIL value-capture from real revenue the centerpiece — the burn/lock question is the whole ballgame.
  • O3 — Survive the SP/security bridge to the Oct-2026 cliff.
  • O4 — Build real distribution — demand is sold, not announced.
The through-lineThe official plan optimizes for getting demand. A turnaround optimizes for getting demand in the one place we can win, converting it into FIL value before the cliff, without dying of supply collapse first. O2 — value capture — is where any serious proposal must plant its flag.
Part VI

The heart of it: does paid demand reach FIL? (No.)

This is the existential question. The answer, from the mechanics, is unambiguous: FIL captures essentially no value from paid usage today. Every live FIL lock/burn is keyed to committed capacity, faults, or congestion — not paid demand. When a client pays via Filecoin Pay in USDFC/USDC, the stablecoin settles to the provider and FIL is never touched; the SP then often sells FIL for opex — net sell-pressure.

Part VIII

How the payment economy actually works

Before designing, we read the machinery — and the Filecoin Pay source code surfaced two findings that reshape the design.

Finding 1 — the base burn already existsFilecoin Pay already charges a hard-coded 0.5% network fee, burned to FIL — and for stablecoins it accumulates and is converted to FIL via a Dutch auction and burned. The "swap-to-FIL-and-burn" path partly ships today (and is non-upgradeable). A proposal must go beyond it, not reinvent it.
Finding 2 — two economies barely touchA FIL-native protocol economy (rewards, pledge, burns — all supply-side) and a commercial economy (clients paying providers in stablecoin) are structurally decoupled. The client's dollar never becomes FIL beyond the 0.5% + gas.

Part X

The design: the FIL Value Engine

Designing the staking layer revealed the sharpening that makes the whole thing coherent: the provider's opt-in "capture" cut IS an SLA-insurance premium. The mechanism collapses from "an abstract value skim + staking" into a real product.

The conceptAn on-chain SLA-insurance market: SPs/processors pay a premium (% of paid revenue) to offer funded SLA guarantees to clients. The premium (a) burns FIL (scarcity) and (b) pays FIL stakers who underwrite the guarantees (a claim on network revenue). FIL captures value as the byproduct of a real product — not a circular skim.

Why it's the right Area-4 mechanism: it goes beyond the existing 0.5% burn; it's non-circular (FIL bought with external stablecoin revenue); it has a real adoption logic (providers pay because they sell funded SLAs); it's solvent by construction (bounded payouts, SP-first-loss, correlation-aware capital, reserve tranche — the FIP-0098 "uninsurable" lesson); and it clears every Area-4 exclusion (counterparty-plural, app-layer mainnet v1 + FIP track, structural & automated).

Part XI

The economic model

The premium must price the correlated tail (mass SP failure), not the average fault rate — that's the solvency crux. Illustratively: premium p ≈ 3% of paid revenue, split 50% burn / 40% staker yield / 10% reserve (buffer-first). Staking finds equilibrium where yield-implied APY meets stakers' required return: staked FIL = yield ÷ APY.

At $100M revenuePremium pool🔥 Burn/yr🔒 FIL staked (8% APY)

At $500M, ~86M FIL staked — comparable to the entire current SP pledge. Solvency holds via coverage ≤ gearing × (stake + reserve + SP bonds) with conservative gearing (~1×, not Nexus's 4.8×); you never sell more coverage than capacity. The optimization maximizes FIL captured × volume retained subject to solvency — so early p stays low (nascent demand) and the proposal sells the compounding curve, not a big day-1 take.

Conclusion

The through-line

Filecoin's 2026 turnaround rests on one unsolved problem: paid demand does not reach FIL. The strategy's objectives are right in spirit but unfocused; the fashionable wedges are narrower than the hype; the network's defensible place is verifiable persistence, not the cloud. The grant program — and the value-capture analysis — converge on the same answer: a mechanism that turns external, stablecoin-denominated paid revenue into FIL value, structurally and non-circularly.

The answerThe FIL Value Engine — a funded, on-chain SLA-insurance market whose premiums burn FIL and pay FIL stakers who underwrite service quality — is the mechanism that makes the entire 2026 strategy actually accrue to the token. It is the rung at the bottom of the ladder, and it is load-bearing.
